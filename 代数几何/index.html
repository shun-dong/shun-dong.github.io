代数几何

仿射空间与射影空间

仿射空间 $(\mathbb{A}:Set,V:Vect,\+\:V\to \operatorname{Aut}\mathbb{A})$ s.t. $T$ is simple transitive, $T_1=1,T_vT_w=T_{v+w}$
For $PQ:\mathbb{A}$, $P-Q:=\vec{QP}:=v:Vs.t.~v+Q=P$
可以看作 V 忘掉原点，无法定义加法和数乘
平行依然是不变量，因此不存在无穷远元素或者认为无穷远元素是特殊的
$v+V$ 不是一个向量空间因为对加法和数乘不封闭，但是是仿射空间，因此被称为仿射集

射影空间 $\mathbb{P}V:=V^*/k$, $\mathbb{P}^n_{k}:=\mathbb{P}k^{n+1}$ 其中坐标记为 $[x_0:x_1:\dots:x_n]$
同样没有特殊的原点，因为没有 $[0:0\dots:0]$, 并且任何点都可以选择一个仿射集，使得在这个仿射集上投影到任何点（因此即使选择了这个仿射集上的原点也没用）
不再有特殊的无穷远元素

[!example] Riemann sphere
$\Sigma:=\mathbb{P}^1_{\mathbb{C}}\simeq \mathbb{S}^2$

设$k[x_0, x_1, \dots, x_n]$是$n+1$元多项式环，若多项式$f$满足$f(tx_0, tx_1, \dots, tx_n) = t^d f(x_0, \dots, x_n)$（$\forall t \in k^*$，$d \geq 0$为次数），则称$f$为d次齐次多项式。

代数簇

[!definition] 代数集(algebraic set)
设$k[x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n]$是代数闭域$k$上的$n$元多项式环，$S \subseteq k[x_1, \dots, x_n]$是多项式(resp. 齐次多项式)集合。定义$S$在$n$维仿射空间$\mathbb{A}^n_k$(resp. $n$维射影空间$\mathbb{P}^n_k$)中的零点集为代数集. $$
V(S) = { P \in \mathbb{A}^n_k (resp.\mathbb{P}^n_k)\mid f(P) = 0 \quad \forall f \in S }
$$

In GTM52, they use the notation $Z(S)$.

[!example]
For $y-x^2\in k[x,y]$, $V(y-x^2)$ is a parabolic.

[!proposition] $V(S)=V(\left< S \right>)$

[!proposition]

  1. The union of two algebraic sets is an algebraic set.
  2. The intersection of algebraic sets is an algebraic set.
  3. The empty space and the whole space are algebraic sets.

$idea.$
Let $Y_1:=V(S_1)$, $Y_2:=V(S_2)$, $Y_{\alpha}:=V(S_\alpha)$,
$Y_1\cup Y_2=Z(S_1S_2)$, $\bigcap_{\alpha}Y_{\alpha}=V\left( \bigcup S_{\alpha} \right)$.
$\varnothing=V(1),\mathbb{A}^n_k=V(0)$

[!definition] Zariski topology
The open sets are the complements of the algebraic sets.

[!example] Zariski topology of $\mathbb{A}^1$
$k[z]$ is a PID $\implies$ ideals are of form $\left< f(z) \right>$
$k$ is algebraically close $\implies$ $f(z)=c(z-a_1)\dots(z-a_n)$
$\implies V(\left< f(z) \right>)=\left{ a_1\dots a_n \right}$ i.e. finite sets
$k$ is algebraically close $\implies$ $\mathbb{A}^1$ is infinite
(for $\mathbb{F}_q$, there exists infinite many irreducible polynomials but finite first order polynomials, so it is not algebraically clossed.)
For $U,V\in Open(\mathbb{A}^1)$, $U\cap V$ is infinite $\implies$ $\mathbb{A}^1$ is not Haustoff.

[!definition] irreducible
For $X:\mathbf{Top}$, non-empty $Y\subseteq X$ is said to be irreducible $\iff \not\exists proper~Y_1,Y_2$ being close w.r.t $Y$ and $Y_1\cup Y_2=Y$.

[!proposition] irreducible
Y is irreducible in X $\implies \bar Y$ is irreducible in X.

$Proof.$ If $\bar Y=Y_1\cup Y_2$ are closed, because $Y$ is closed, then one of them includes Y.
close $\implies \bar Y\subseteq Y_i$
$\blacksquare$

[!example] irreducible sets w.r.t. $\mathbb{A}^1$
The close sets are finite.

[!proposition]
Any non-empty open subset of an irreducible space is dense and irriducile.

$Proof.$
(dense)
Suppose not, let $U$ s.t. $O\cap U=\varnothing$. Then $X=O^c\cup U^c$. contradiction.
(irreducible)
Suppose not, let $O=U\cup V$, where $U,V$ are closed subsets. Then $O$ is closed. contradiction.
$\blacksquare$

[!definition] affine (algebraic) variety
We call irreducible closed subsets of $\mathbb{A}^n$ affine (algebraic) varieties.
And we called irreducible open subsets quasi-affine varieties.

[!definition] 点集的消失理想
对任意子集 $X\subset \mathbb{P}^n_kor\mathbb{A}^n_k$, 定义
$$

I(X)={f\in k[x_1,\dots,x_n]\mid f(p)=0\text{ for all }p\in X}.

$$

它称为 $X$ 的消失理想 (vanishing ideal).

^def-vanishing-ideal

For $I\unlhd R$, radical ideal (根理想) $\sqrt I:=\left{ a\in I\mid \exists n\in \mathbb{N},\exists b\in R,b^n=a \right}\unlhd R$
The prime ideals are radical. For $r^n\in \mathfrak{p}$, $r\in \mathfrak{p}$.

[!proposition] 零点只看根理想
对任意理想 $I\subset k[x_1,\dots,x_n]$, 有
$$

V(I)=V(\sqrt I).

$$

$Proof.$
($V(I)\subseteq V(\sqrt I)$)
For any $p\in V(I)$, $f\in V(\sqrt I)$, there exists $m_f$ s.t. $f^{m_f}=g$ for some $g\in V(I)$ $\implies f(p)^{m_f}=0$.
$k$ is an integer domain $\implies f(p)=0$.
$\implies p\in V(\sqrt I)$
($V(I)\supseteq V(\sqrt I)$)
由 $I\subset\sqrt I$
$\blacksquare$

[!proposition]

  1. If $S_1\subseteq S_2\subseteq A$, then $V(S_1)\supseteq V(S_2)$.
  2. If $Y_1\subseteq Y_2\subseteq \mathbb{A}^n$, then $I(Y_1)\supseteq I(Y_2)$.
  3. For $Y_{\alpha}\subseteq \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{A}^n)$, $\bigcap_{\alpha}I(Y_\alpha)=I(\bigcup_{\alpha}Y_\alpha)$.
  4. For $I\unlhd A$, $I(V(I))=\sqrt I$.
  5. For $Y\subseteq \mathbb{A}^n$, $V(I(Y))=\bar Y$.

$Proof.$
4. By [[交换代数#Hilbert_零点定理]]
5. $V(I(Y))$ is close and $Y\subseteq V(I(Y))$ $\implies \bar Y\subseteq V(I(Y))$. Let $W$ be any close set containing $Y$. Then $W=V(I)$ for some ideal $I$. So $Y\subseteq V(I)$, and $I(V(I))\subseteq I(Y)$. We know that $I\subseteq I(V(I))$ $\implies W\supseteq V(I(Y))$.
$\blacksquare$

[!corollary]
$$
\begin{align}\left{ algebraicsets \right} & \cong \left{ radicalideals \right} \
Y & \mapsto I(Y) \
V(I) & \gets!!!| I\end{align}
$$
And it inverses inclusion.

[!proposition]
$Y$ is irreducible $\iff$ $I(Y)$ is a prime ideal

$Proof.$
($\implies$) For $fg\in I(Y)$, $V(f)\cup V(g)=V(fg)\supseteq V(I(Y))=Y$. So $Y=(Y\cap V(f))\cup (Y\cap V(g))$, which are closed sets in $Y$. Because $Y$ is irreducible, one of them is equal to $Y$ i.e. $Y\subseteq V(f)orV(g)$. By definition, $forg\in I(Y)$.
($\impliedby$) Let $\mathfrak{p}=I(Y)$. If $V(\mathfrak{p})=Y_1\cup Y_2$ are closed, then $\mathfrak{p}=I(Y_1)\cap I(Y_2)$. So $I(Y_1)=\mathfrak{p}$ or $I(Y_2)=\mathfrak{p}$ (for $\mathfrak{p}=I_1\cap I_2\supseteq I_1I_2$, if there exist $f\in I_1-I_2,g\in I_2-I_1$, $fg\in \mathfrak{p}$ $\implies f\in \mathfrak{p}\vee g\in \mathfrak{p}$. contradiction.) Thus $V(\mathfrak{p})=Y_1orY_2$.
$\blacksquare$

[!corollary]
$$
\begin{align}\left{ algebraic~varieties \right} & \cong \operatorname{Spec}A\
Y & \mapsto I(Y) \
V(I) & \gets!!!| I\end{align}
$$
And it inverses inclusion.

[!example]
Suppose $f$ being an irreducible polynomial in $k[X]:=k[x_1,\dots x_n]$, because $k[X]$ is a UFD, $\left< f \right>$ is a prime ideal. So $V(f)$ is an affine variety. We call it an affine curve($n=2$), surface($n=3$), hypersurface($n>3$).

[!proposition]
$$

\begin{align}
k^n & \cong\operatorname{MaxSpec}k[X] \
(a_1,\dots a_n) & \mapsto \left< x_1-a_1,\dots x_n-a_n \right>
\end{align}
$$

$Proof.$
Let $I=\left< x_1-a_1,\dots x_n-a_n \right>$, $k[X]/I\cong k$ is a field $\implies I$ is an maximal ideal.
For maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ of $k[X]$, by the inclusion-inversion property, it is generated by a minimal (under $\subseteq$) variety of $\mathbb{A}^n$, which is a point.
$\blacksquare$

[!remark]
If $k$ is not algebraically closed, these results do not hold.
For example, in $\mathbb{A}_{\mathbb{R}}^2$, $V(x^2+y^2+1)=\varnothing$.

[!definition] 仿射坐标环(affine coordinate ring)
For affine algebraic set $Y$, 它的坐标环 (coordinate ring) 是
$$

k[ Y]=k[x_1,\dots,x_n]/I(Y).

$$

GTM 52 uses notation $A(Y)$ where $A=k[X]$.

[!remark]
For affine variety $Y$, $k[Y]$ is an integer domain and a finitely generated $k$-algebra. Any finitely generated $k$-algebra as well as domain can be written as $k[X]/I$ where $I$ is a prime ideal, which implies $I=I(Y)$ for some affine variety.

[!definition] Noetherian topological space
If the closed subsets satisfied descending chain condition, we call the space Noetherian topological space.

[!example]
$\mathbb{A}^n$ is a Noetherian topological space. For close $Y_1\supseteq Y_2 \supseteq Y_3\dots$, $I(Y_1)\subseteq I(Y_2)\subseteq\dots$. $k[X]$ is a Noetherian ring $\implies$ $I(Y_i)$ is eventually stable $\implies$ $Y_i=V(I(Y_i))$ is eventually stable.

[!proposition]
In a Noetherian topological space $X$, every non-empty closed subset $Y$ can be written as a finite union of irreducible closed subsets $Y=\bigcup_{i=1}^rY_i$.
If we require $Y_i$ are not contained in another, then $\left{ Y_i \right}$ is unique. They are called the irreducible components of $Y$.

$Proof.$
(existence)
Let $\mathfrak{S}$ be set of non-empty closed subsets that do not satisfy such condition. Suppose.$\mathfrak{S}\neq\varnothing$. Noetherian $\implies$ there exists minimal $Y\in \mathfrak{S}$ under $\subseteq$. There exists close $Y_1,Y_2$ s.t. $Y_1\cup Y_2=Y$, for example $Y_1=Y_2=Y$. Because $Y$ is not irreducible, we can find $Y_1,Y_2\neq Y$. By minimality of $Y$, $Y_1,Y_2$ can be written as finite union of irreducible closed subsets. contradiction.
(uniqueness)
Suppose $Y=\bigcup_{i=1}^m Y_i=\bigcup_{j=1}^n Y’_j$ are irreducible compositions. $\implies$ $Y_1’=\bigcup_{i=1}^m(Y_1’\cap Y_{i})$. Because $Y’_1$ is irreducible, $Y’_1\subseteq Y_i$ for some i. Similarly $Y_i\subseteq Y_j’$ for some j.Then $Y_1’\subseteq Y’_j$ $\implies j=1$ and $Y_i=Y_1’$…
$\blacksquare$

[!definition] dimension of topological space
For $X:\mathbf{Top}$, we define the dimension $\dim X$ to be the supremum of n s.t. $Z_0 \subset Z_1\subset \dots\subset Z_n$ of irriducible closed subsets of $X$.

[!example]
$\dim \mathbb{A}^1=1$ because the irreducible closed subsets of $\mathbb{A}^1$ are.only the whole space of a single points.

[!definition] the height of a prime ideal
In a ring $A$, the height of a prime ideal $\operatorname{height}\mathfrak{p}$ is the superimum of n s.t. $\mathfrak{p}_0\subset \mathfrak{p}_1\dots\subset \mathfrak{p}_n=\mathfrak{p}$ of prime ideals.
The Krull dimension of $A$ is defined to be the supremum of the heights of prime ideals.

The Krull dimension can be seem as the topological dimension of $\operatorname{Spec}A$.

[!proposition] Krull dimension
For an affine algebraic set $Y$, the topological dimension is equal to the Krull dimension of $k[Y]$.

$Proof.$
By $\left{ affine~varieties\right}\cong\operatorname{Spec}k[X]$, for affine variety $Z$ s.t. $Z\subseteq Y$, $I(Y)\subseteq I(Z)$. By the corresponding thm, $I(Z)$ can be seem as an element of $\operatorname{Spec}k[X]/I(Y)=\operatorname{Spec}k[Y]$.
$\blacksquare$

[!theorem]
Let $k$ be a field, $B$ be an integral domain as well as a finitely generated $k$-algebra.

  1. $\dim B=\deg_kK(B)$, where RHS is the transcendence degree of the quotient field $K(B)$.
  2. For $\mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}B$, $\operatorname{height}\mathfrak{p}+\dim B/\mathfrak{p}=\dim B$.

$Proof.$ By [[交换代数]]。

[!proposition] $\dim \mathbb{A}^n=n$

$Proof.$
The coordinate ring of $\mathbb{A}^n$ is $k[X]/I(\mathbb{A}^n)=k[X]/\mathfrak{0}=k[X]$
By [[#^abasd]], $\dim k[X]=n$.
$\blacksquare$

[!proposition]
For quasi-affine variety $Y$, $\dim Y = \dim \bar Y$.

$Proof.$
For irreducible closed subsets in.$Y$ $Z_0\subset Z_1\dots\subset Z_n$, $\bar Z_0\subset \bar Z_1\dots\subset\bar Z_n$ are irreducible closed subsets in.$\bar Y$ (notice that the closure are taken in $\bar Y$. For irreducible subset, the last element of the maximal chain is itself.) $\implies \dim Y\leq \dim \bar Y$.
If $\dim Y$ is finite, let $Z_0\subset Z_1\subset \dots Z_n$ is a maximal chain.

[!theorem] Krull’s Hauptidealsatz
Let A be a noetherian ring, and let $f\in A$ be an element which is neither a zero divisor nor a unit. Then every minimal prime ideal p containing f has height 1.

[!proposition]
A noetherian integral domain A is a unique factorization domain if and only if every prime ideal of height 1 is principal.

[!proposition]
A variety $Y$ in A” has dimension n - 1 if and only if it is the zero set Z(f) of a single nonconstant irreducible polynomial in $A =k[x_1, \dots ,x_n]$.

[!remark]
A prime ideal of height 2 in a polynomial ring cannot necessarily be generated by two elements.

[!definition] presheaf & sheaf
For $X:\mathbf{Top},\mathcal{C}:\mathbf{Cat}$, $\mathcal{C}_X^{pre}:=Open_X^{op}\to C$ is called the category of presheaves of $\mathcal{C}$ of $X$, and $\mathscr{F}:\mathcal{C}^{pre}_X$ is called a presheaf of $\mathcal{C}$ of $X$.
Under the above assumptions, if $$
\bullet\to \mathscr{F}(U)\to\prod_i\mathscr{F}(U_i)\rightrightarrows\prod_{i~j} \mathscr{F}(U_i\cap U_j)
$$ is exact, then we call $\mathscr{F}$ a sheaf. The category of sheaves of $\mathcal{C}$ of $X$ is defined as a full subcategory of the category of presheaves of $\mathcal{C}$ of $X$.

[!definition] stalk
For $\mathscr{F}:\mathcal{C}^{pre}_X$, $p\in X$, the stalks at $p$ is defined as $\mathscr{F}_p:=\underset{\longrightarrow}{\operatorname{lim}}{}_{U:Open_X,p\in U}\mathscr{F}(U)$. If $\mathcal{C}$ is a category of sets with some structure, $s:\mathscr{F}$ is called a section, and $s_p$ is defined as the image of $s$ in $\mathscr{F}_p$ and is called a germ.

[!definition] space of sections a.k.a. espace étalé
Under the above assumptions, define $Et(\mathscr{F}):\mathbf{Top}$ as follows. $Set(Et(\mathscr{F})):=\coprod_{p\in X}\mathscr{F}_p$, and $\left{ [s_p]_{p\in U}\mid U: Open_X,s\in \mathscr{F}(U) \right}$ is a basis of topology of $Et(\mathscr{F})$.

[!definition] sheafification
The sheafification functor is the left-adjoint to the forgetful functor $U:\mathcal{C}_X\to \mathcal{C}^{pre}_X$ i.e.

commutes. The concrete construction is the selections of $\pi:\coprod_{p\in X}\mathscr{F}_p$.

[!definition] 素谱
For commutative ring $A$, $\operatorname{Spec}A:=\left{\mathfrak{p}\subseteq A\mid \mathfrak{p}isaprimeideal \right}$

[!definition] 剩余域
For $\mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}A$, $\kappa(\mathfrak{p}):=A_\mathfrak{p}/\mathfrak{p}A_\mathfrak{p}$, 这里 $A_\mathfrak{p}:=(A-\mathfrak{p})^{-1}A$ 表示局部化. 诱导出赋值映射 $\iota_\mathfrak{p}:A\to\kappa(\mathfrak{p})$, 不引起歧义的情况下也可以记为 $f(\mathfrak{p}):=\iota_\mathfrak{p}f$

[!example]
$\operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{C}[z]=\left{ \left< z-c \right> \mid c\in \mathbb{C}\right}\cup \left{ \left< 0 \right> \right}$
For $f\in \mathbb{C}[z]$, $f(\left< z-c \right>)=f(c)\in \mathbb{C}$, $f(\left< 0 \right>)=f(z)\in \mathbb{C}(z)$

但一个函数在所有点的赋值为 0 不一定是 零函数, 实际上只能推出属于 $\bigcap_{\mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}A}\mathfrak{p}=\sqrt {\left< 0 \right>}$ , 比如在 $\mathbb{C}[z]/\left< z^2 \right>$ 中 $z$ 就是幂零元.

[!definition] Zariski 拓扑
对 $S\subseteq A$, $V(S):=\left{ \mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}A\mid \forall f\in S,\iota_{\mathfrak{p}}f=0 \right}$
定义 Zariski 拓扑 的闭集族为 $\left{ V(I) \right}_{I\unlhd A}$
或者定义拓扑基 $D(f)=\operatorname{Spec}A-V(f)$

注意到 $$
\begin{align}
V(I) & =\left{ \mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}A\mid \forall f\in I,\iota_{\mathfrak{p}}f=0 \right} \
& =\left{ \mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}A\mid \forall f\in I,f\in \mathfrak{p} \right} \
& =\left{ \mathfrak{p}\in \operatorname{Spec}A\mid I\subseteq \mathfrak{p} \right} \
\end{align}
$$
$\operatorname{Spec}A=V(\varnothing)$, $\varnothing=V(A)$.
